## 1. What This Is

Matic AI Ecommerce is a **managed ecommerce service** for independent retailers — physical store owners, live streamers, and creator-sellers — who need a professional online store but don't have the budget for an agency, the time to learn Shopify, or the technical skill to build something themselves.

You get a custom-built storefront that looks like it cost $20,000, operated on an ongoing basis by Matic AI using a suite of AI tools to continuously improve it — without ever needing to hire a developer or a marketing agency.

**The one-line pitch:**  
*"We build your store, we run your store, and we make it sell."*

---

## 2. The Problem

Independent retailers — especially those selling through physical locations and live streaming platforms like Whatnot, TikTok Shop, and Instagram Live — are leaving money on the table every day because:

- They have no permanent home base online. Their customers have nowhere to go between streams.
- Their current "store" is a Linktree, a Shopify template they set up in an afternoon, or just DMs.
- Managing inventory across a physical store, a live stream, and an online shop is chaotic and entirely manual.
- They can't afford a developer for every update. A simple banner change on Shopify can cost $200+ from a freelancer.
- They're building an audience on platforms they don't own. If Whatnot changes its algorithm or fees, their business evaporates.

The market has two options right now: DIY platforms like Shopify (cheap but time-consuming and technical) or full-service agencies (professional but $5,000–$50,000 to start). There is nothing in between for the independent seller doing $5K–$100K/month.

**Matic AI Ecommerce lives in that gap.**

---

## 3. The Solution

A fully managed ecommerce service delivered in two phases:

**Phase 1 — Build (one-time setup)**  
A bespoke storefront designed specifically for the merchant's brand, audience, and product type. Built on a modern, fast, SEO-optimized stack. Completed in 2–3 weeks. Includes everything needed to start selling online immediately.

**Phase 2 — Operate (monthly retainer)**  
Ongoing AI-powered management of the store — product updates, email campaigns, live drop pages, SEO improvements, A/B testing, analytics review, and anything else that keeps the store growing. The merchant focuses on their product and their audience. Matic AI handles the store.

## 6. What "AI-Powered Management" Actually Means

This is the core of the Matic AI value proposition and what separates it from both DIY and traditional agencies. Here is what AI enables operationally:

**Product descriptions:** Claude generates SEO-optimized, on-brand product descriptions in seconds. What would take a copywriter 30 minutes per product takes 30 seconds. This makes it economically viable to keep every listing fresh and well-written.

**Email campaigns:** Campaign copy (subject line, body, CTA) is drafted by Claude using the merchant's brand voice, product details, and audience context. Rafael reviews and sends. One email campaign that would take an agency a full day takes under an hour.

**SEO:** Meta titles, descriptions, alt text, and collection page copy are generated and updated monthly using Claude. No SEO agency needed.

**A/B testing:** Variant headlines, hero copy, and CTA text are generated by Claude, implemented as variants, and the winner is shipped when statistically clear. No conversion rate agency needed.

**Live drop pages:** When a stream is scheduled, a dedicated landing page is created — headline, product grid, countdown timer, stream embed — using a template system that Claude populates with the merchant's details in minutes.

**Analytics interpretation:** Raw Supabase and Stripe data is summarized into plain-English insights by Claude. Rafael reviews and sends a brief monthly summary to the merchant. No analytics consultant needed.

**The result:** Rafael can manage 5–10 merchant accounts at the retainer rate with the output quality of a full-service agency, because AI handles the production work while Rafael handles strategy, relationships, and quality control.

## 11. Pricing Rationale

**Why $1,500 setup?**
A Shopify freelancer charges $75–$150/hour. A custom storefront build is realistically 20–40 hours of work. At $1,500, the merchant is getting a steal and Matic AI is making a reasonable return only because AI compresses build time significantly. It's also low enough that it's an easy yes for a seller doing even $5K/month.

**Why $150–$500/month?**
The cheapest Shopify plan is $39/month — but that gives you nothing except the platform. Every app, every theme, every feature costs extra. A merchant on Shopify paying for: theme ($180/year) + email app ($30/month) + review app ($15/month) + upsell app ($20/month) is already at $65–$80/month with zero management. Matic AI at $150/month gives them all of that plus someone actually running it.

**When to raise prices:**
Once there are 3+ active clients and the process is proven, the Grow tier should move to $350/month and Scale to $600/month. The current pricing is intentionally slightly below market to close the first few clients and build case studies.

## 12. Sales Process

### Step 1 — Warm intro or outreach
Lead comes through a mutual connection, Instagram DM, or referral. First touch is a short intro message explaining what Matic AI Ecommerce does in one sentence and asking if they're open to a quick call.

### Step 2 — Discovery call (20–30 min)
Goal: understand their situation, not pitch features. Key questions:
- How are you selling right now and what's broken about it?
- Do you have a website? What do you wish it did that it doesn't?
- How much time do you spend on store updates vs. actually streaming/selling?
- What would it mean for your business if you had 500 people visiting your store the week after a big stream?

End the call by offering to show them a demo store built specifically for their category.

### Step 3 — Demo presentation
Show the Chingle Grumpus demo (or a relevant demo for their category). Walk through:
1. Homepage — the brand, the energy, the mascot integration
2. Shop page — product catalog, filters
3. Live drop page — the countdown, the live activation
4. Giveaway page — the email capture, the community building
5. Checkout — actually complete a test purchase in front of them
6. "This is what we could build for you — but for [their brand]."

### Step 4 — Proposal
Send a 1-page proposal within 24 hours of the demo. Include:
- Recommended tier and what's included
- Timeline (2–3 weeks to launch)
- Pricing (setup + monthly)
- Simple SOW: deliverables, revision policy, payment terms
- "Reply YES to get started" — frictionless close

### Step 5 — Onboarding
Collect 50% of setup fee upfront. Send brand intake form. Schedule discovery call. Build begins.

## 13. What This Is Not

To be clear about scope — especially in early sales conversations:

- **Not a Shopify replacement for enterprise retailers.** This is for independent sellers doing under $1M/year.
- **Not fully automated (yet).** Rafael operates the AI tools. The merchant is buying Rafael's time + AI leverage, not a software product they log into.
- **Not an inventory management system.** Matic AI manages the online store. Physical inventory tracking is the merchant's responsibility (for now).
- **Not a social media management agency.** Copy support on Scale tier is for product launches only — not full social media management.
- **Not a Whatnot replacement.** The store complements the merchant's Whatnot presence, it doesn't replace it. Whatnot is where they stream and build audience. The store is where that audience can buy anytime.

## 14. Future Roadmap (Post-Launch)

Once the service is proven with 3–5 paying clients, the following expansions become viable:

**Near-term (3–6 months)**
- Real email delivery integration (Resend or Klaviyo) — enables actual campaign sends, not just drafts
- Admin dashboard for merchants — simple view of orders, signups, and live drop status without needing to contact Matic AI
- Giveaway winner selection automation — random selection from Supabase entries, announcement template

**Medium-term (6–12 months)**
- Standardized store template library — reduce build time from 2–3 weeks to 3–5 days, enabling lower setup fee or higher margin
- Whatnot / TikTok Shop inventory sync — pull live inventory into the store automatically after streams
- Expand to adjacent niches — sports cards, sneakers, vintage clothing, handmade goods, local food brands

**Long-term (12–24 months)**
- Merchant self-serve portal — merchants log in to see analytics, trigger email sends, update products themselves (moving toward a hybrid managed/SaaS model)
- White-label option — other freelancers or small agencies license the Matic AI Ecommerce stack and process
- Potential productization into a true SaaS offering if client volume and template standardization supports it
